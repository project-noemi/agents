# Phase 0 Assessment — Practitioner Notes

This document is for **MSPs, MSSPs, and NoéMI program participants** who perform Phase 0 assessments on behalf of clients. The assessment documents themselves ([security-assessment.md](security-assessment.md), [ai-readiness-assessment.md](ai-readiness-assessment.md)) are written for a general audience including business owners. This document contains positioning guidance, delivery tips, and framing advice for practitioners.

## Positioning the Security Assessment

The security assessment is not "extra overhead." It protects the value of the AI initiative itself.

When speaking with a business owner, the core message is:

> We are reducing the chance that your AI project creates new operational risk faster than it creates value.

The business value to emphasize:

- fewer surprises after launch
- lower chance of data leakage or uncontrolled Shadow AI behavior
- less rework caused by rushed, unsafe implementations
- clearer approval and accountability before the workflow touches sensitive systems
- higher confidence that a future pilot can scale instead of being shut down by risk

When presenting the security assessment, the simplest executive summary is:

> The security assessment tells us whether the organization is grounded enough to start AI safely, what must be fixed first, and what controls must remain in place as the initiative grows.

## Positioning the AI Readiness Assessment

The AI readiness assessment helps practitioners speak like operators, not tool users.

The core value statement is:

> We are not just helping your team do the task faster. We are helping your team redesign the workflow so AI handles repeatable first-pass work while your people manage approvals, exceptions, and quality.

That matters because the business outcome is bigger than convenience:

- more output with the same team
- faster turnaround on repetitive work
- more consistent first drafts or first-pass decisions
- less skilled human time spent on low-leverage repetition
- clearer path to measurable ROI

When presenting the AI readiness assessment, the simplest executive summary is:

> The AI readiness assessment tells us whether this organization can turn AI into governed output, measurable productivity, and lower delivery cost — without confusing experimentation for transformation.

## The NoéMI Value Shift

This split between security and AI readiness helps practitioners speak to business owners at a higher level.

Instead of saying:

> I can do this task with AI.

you can say:

> We can assess whether this workflow is safe, whether it is worth automating, what human role should remain in control, and how the business can increase output without increasing headcount at the same rate.

That is the shift from tool use to governance and value creation.

## How Work Changes With AI — The Role-Uplift Framing

When explaining to a business owner how AI changes the shape of work, use this structure:

- **Before AI:** skilled people spend valuable time doing every repetitive step themselves
- **With governed AI:** AI handles the first-pass work inside defined boundaries
- **After uplift:** people spend more time reviewing, improving, deciding, and governing

This is how the organization moves from labor substitution thinking to **Virtual Workforce** thinking. The team is not just learning to perform the task with AI. It is learning to **manage AI doing the task**.

## Delivery Tips

- Lead with the business problem, not the assessment methodology.
- Use plain language. "Identity posture" means nothing to most business owners. "Who can log into what, and is that controlled properly?" lands better.
- Present findings in terms of business impact, not technical severity scores.
- Always distinguish between blockers and non-blockers. Business owners need to know what stops them from starting versus what they can improve in parallel.
- End with the readiness classification and first pilot recommendation. That is the actionable takeaway.
